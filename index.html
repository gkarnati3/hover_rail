Maintenance and Troubleshooting

          
Making sure you are always able to get where you want to go!

        


        


          

            

              
            

          


          

            

              
The HoverRail system relies on a delicate balance between your person, our infrastructure, and the electromagnetic field. Fortunately, HoverRail leverages its revolutionary QR-code service dispatch system to keep its infrastructure running. If there is an infrastructure issue, you will be routed around the problem.


              
However, it is important to note that bottlenecks do sometimes arise in more remote service areas. In rural regions, infrastructure may go down and not be services for at most a day. Fortunately, our reliable HoverRail infrastructure schedules this maintenance and you will be notified in advance if there is an issue that might arise from this.


              
In almost all cases, any issues encountered during your HoverRail experience are likely due to lack of user maintenance. Some of our most common user-side issues are solved below. If none of these solve your issue, please consult our always-available emergency line.


            


          


        


        

          

            

              
              
Maintenance Tips

            

            
            

              
              
Ingest iron daily

              
It is critical the HoverRail users ingest iron daily so that our S.L.I.M. magnets can exert the proper force on your body. Over time, and with higher doses, metals naturally accumulate in your tissues to make your HoverRail experience as seamless as possible. Users without enough trace metals in their bodies are strongly discouraged from utilizing HoverRail, as there have been cases of "forced trace metal extraction" in these cases. Users just beginning to accumulate metals might experience nausea when first riding HoverRail due to the forces exerted by their trace metals on their tissues. However, more experienced users eventually enjoy smooth rides as metals evenly embed themselves in their bodies, so the tissue force distribution becomes lesser and more evenly spread.

            


            

              
              
Perform light physical activity before boarding

              
Performing basic calisthenic exercises like jumping-jacks and push-ups can make you a more sustainable HoverRail rider. Light calisthenic exercise swirls the trace metals around in the body, like shaking a snowglobe. This helps break up metals embedded deep in tissues that naturally accumulate, reducing the risk of long-term tissue damage.

            

            
            

              
              
Store any metallic items in a Faraday box

              
Users are encouraged to stow any metallic objects larger than a wedding ring in a personal Faraday box before boarding a HoverRail. HoverRail's S.L.I.M. magnets operate using very strong magnetic fields, so metallic objects must be electromagnetically isolated. Otherwise, users risk side effects ranging from loss of property to serious injury or death.

            


          


          

              

                
                
Troubleshooting Tips

              

              
              

                
                
"I cannot levitate"

                
If you cannot levitate on a working HoverRail, your magnetic field is probably not strong enough. Most commonly, this is due to an iron deficiency. It is imperative that HoverRail users ingest at least 50g of iron supplements every week. Free iron level testing is available at most clinics. If you want to fly fast, we recommend an iron blood concentration level of at least 100 μg/mL.

              

  
              

                
                
"I fell off the track"

                
If you find yourself falling off the HoverRail tracks during a ride, please walk to the nearest embarkment station or activation emergency magnetism. We recommend you have emergency magnetism backup on by default in case of a steep fall. Remember that HoverRail denies any liability for injury experienced by users with blood iron concentrations less than 100 μg/mL! For users unable to accomodate such high blood iron concentrations, we recommend investing in a helmet.

              

              
              

                
                
"I cannot stop spinning"

                
Unfortunately, frictionless transport comes at a price. Due to the conservation of rotational energy, users can sometimes accumulate angular momentum from the wind or other sources during long-haul rides. If you find yourself in this situaiton, use your emergency Eddy current brake to absorb the rotational energy. Be sure to brake slowly, since the energy is absorbed as heat, the brake might burn your hands if you brake too quickly.

The Fundamentals

            
Levitation may seem to good to be true, but the advances we've made at Daedalus are rooted in sound scientific phenomena.
              The mechanism behind this technology rests on super diamagnetically stabilized levitation and oscillating electromagnetic fields through linear induction motors. 
              Diamagnetism is a quantum mechanical effect where the material repels magnetic fields. 
              This occurs in all materials but is generally a weaker force compared to other magnetic forces. 
              However, this is not the case with superconductors, which are perfect diamagnets because they expel all magnetic fields.
              This is known as the Meissner effect, where it was observed at sufficiently cold temperatures.
              Since they repel all magnetic fields, a superconductor can repel a weak magnet (i.e. a HoverRail passenger) strongly enough for it to levitate.  
            

            

              Stabilized levitation is possible with superconductors, but to generate thrust, oscillating electromagnetic fields are necessary. 
              This is implemented with linear induction motors, which are alternating current motors that have a flat magnetic core. 
              In a single-sided version, the magnetic field can create repulsion forces that push the object forward, levitating it and carrying it along the direction of the moving magnetic field.
              This technology is used extensively in magnetic propulsion systems such as the magnetic levitation trains in Japan and the Hyperloop.
              Through linear induction motors, a sufficient conductor can be thrusted forward with high energy efficiency.
            

            

              SLIM magnets are a proprietary Daedalus invention that combines the stability of superconductors with the thrust of linear induction motors to make this railway possible. 
              Short for superconductive linear induction motorized magnets, this technology allows weak magnets to hover over the superconductive element, while the alternating currents of the 
              oscillating electric fields propel them forward. These magnets are embedded throughout the railway system, putting the hover in HoverRail.
            

          

        


        

          

            
          


          

            
How Does It All Work?

            

              Since humans are 70% water, which is a weak diamagnet, the superconductor can repel strongly enough for stabilized levitation to be possible. 
              The Meissner effect has been used for levitation on mammals, as researchers at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory to levitate mice and larger water droplets through a superconducting magnet at room temperature.
              In order for the oscillating electric currents to be possible though, the person needs to have a more polarized magnetic field. 
              This is possible, since there is research showing that the human body produces magnetic fields through naturally existing steady currents. 
            

            

              This can be amplified through the ingestion of the HumaLev iron supplement with neodymium, an element with strong polarizing properties. 
              Iron supplements are shown to significantly enrich iron levels throughout the body in anemic and healthy patients. 
              This enrichment coupled with the existing magnetic fields by the human body can be leveraged to polarize a human enough to be thrusted through linear induction motors. 
              Combined with the stabilizing effect of superconducting material, human levitation technology is not only feasible, but a current reality for thousands of HoverRail passengers.
